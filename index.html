Reflection

The amplitude of the function modeling sunlight time can be found by finding the difference between the most and the least amount of sunlight. For Portland, Maine, the aplitude of the function can be found by subtracting 8.9 (the winter solstice) from 15.4 (the summer solstice), then dividing by 2. This results into a difference of 6.5 then divided by 2 to get an amplitude of 3.25.

The inflection points of the functions are the spring and fall equinox. The equinoxes are the points in which the 2nd coefficient stops increasing and starts to decrease. This would be the change in curve, or inflection point. For Portland, Maine, the inflection points would be March 21st and September 21st, the spring and fall equinoxes, where both are at 12.2 hours of sunlight. The inflection points should, for the most part, not change between city to city because the equinoxes are when the Earth is tilted so that all cities get (about) 12 hours of sunlight.

The phase shift, for a sine function with positive amplitude, would be the x value when the y value is at the midline and the graph is increasing. To calculate the phase shift for Portland, Maine, we can find the x value at the midline (one of the equinoxes) where the graph is increasing (spring). This would mean that the phase shift is March 21st, or 80 days. Because we are shifting right, the shift value in our function would be -80.

There are two ways to calculate the midline: adding the amplitude to the minimum or subtracting the amplitude from the maximum. For Melbourne, Australia, that would mean adding 2.65 and 9.5 or subtracting 2.65 from 14.8. This results in a vertical shift of 12.15 for Melbourne, Australia.

The positions of the two cities impact their functions in several ways. The latitude of the cities impact the amplitude of the function. The latitude can also impact the phase shift of the functions, especially in the two hemispheres. The longitude of the city doesn't impact the function as much, but it can change the phase shift slightly. The vertical shift and period will remain constant despite the location of the city.

One observation of the two graphs is that they are almost the same graph, but reflected over the midline. This could be because the amplitudes are similar because the latitudes of the two cities are opposite to each other over the equator. The two cities are reflected over the midline because the cities are in two seperate hemispheres. This would mean that one city would be in winter while the other is in summer.

To calculate the hours of sunlight during my birthday, we have to first find how many days are between December 31st, 2020 and June 13th, 2021. Afterwards, we only have to plug in the days, 164, into our two sunlight equations. For Portland, Maine, the equation would be 3.25\sin\left(\frac{2\pi}{365}\left(164-80\right)\right)+12.15 which evaluates to 15.374 hours of sunlight on June 13th, 2021. For Melbourne, Australia, the equation would be 2.65\sin\left(\frac{2\pi}{365}\left(164-264\right)\right)+12.15 which evaluates to 9.53 hours of sunlight on June 13th, 2021. One important observation here is that the first value is above 12.15 and the second value is below 12.15.
